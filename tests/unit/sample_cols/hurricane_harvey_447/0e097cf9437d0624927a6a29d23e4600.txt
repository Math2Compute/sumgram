Updated at 11:10 p.m.
The eye of Category 4 Hurricane Harvey is now over Aransas Bay. A station at Aransas Pass run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 102 mph with a gust to 132 mph. A station at Aransas Wildlife Refuge run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 75 mph with a gust to 99 mph. A station at Rockport reported a pressure of 945 mb on the western side of the eye.
Updated at 10 p.m.
Eye of Category 4 Hurricane Harvey makes landfall between Port Aransas and Port O'Connor. Catastrophic flooding expected due to heavy rainfall and storm surge.
Updated at 9 p.m.
Eye of Category 4 Hurricane Harvey is almost onshore. A station at Aransas Pass run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 102 mph with a gust to 120 mph. A station at Aransas Wildlife Refuge run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 71 mph  with a gust to 102 mph.
Updated at 8 p.m.
The eyewall of Category 4 Hurricane Harvey is beginning to move onshore on the middle Texas coast. A station at Aransas Pass run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 90 mph with a gust to 108 mph. A station at Flour Bluff recently reported a sustained wind of 81 mph.
Updated at 7:05 p.m.
Eye of Category 4 Hurricane Harvey approaching the coast between Port Aransas and Port O'Connor. Catastrophic flooding expected due to heavy rainfall and storm surge.
Updated at 6 p.m.
Hurricane Harvey has become a Category 4 storm with maximum sustained winds of 130 mph. Sustained hurricane-force winds are spreading onto the middle Texas coast. A station at Aransas Pass run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 74 mph with a gust to 96 mph.
Updated at 5 p.m.
Sustained hurricane-force winds about to move onshore. A station at Aransas Pass run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 67 mph with a gust to 81 mph.
Updated at 4 p.m.
Hurricane Harvey bearing down on the Texas Coast. Catastrophic flooding expected due to heavy rainfall and storm surge. Maximum sustained winds of 125 mph. The storm continues to move northwest at 10 mph and is 60 miles from reaching landfall.
Updated at 3 p.m.
Corpus Christi Naval Air Station recently reported a sustained wind of 53 mph with a gust to 64 mph. A station at Aransas Pass run by the Texas Coastal Observing Network recently reported a sustained wind of 53 mph  with a gust to 68 mph.
Updated at 2:20 p.m.
Hurricane Harvey has strenghthened to a Category 3 storm. It continues to move northwest along the Texas coast at 10 mph., with maximum sustained winds of about 120 mph.
Updated at 11:55 a.m.
The National Weather Service reports that Hurricane Harvey remains a Category 2 storm with wind gusts up to 110 mph. It is currently 100 miles southeast of Corpus Christi and moving slowly northwest and is expected to make landfall on the mid-coast between Corpus Christi and Port O'Connor about midnight. Once the storm reaches land, it is expected to slow down and stall in the Victoria area. About 20 to 30 inches of rain is expected, and flash flood warnings will be in effect through Monday, according to John Metz of the National Weather Service in Corpus Christi.
