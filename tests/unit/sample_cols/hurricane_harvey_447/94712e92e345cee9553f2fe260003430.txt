Hurricane Harvey Weakens Over Land, Barely a Hurricane- UPDATED STORM INFORMATION
Posted:
ablaser@kristv.com
UPDATE: 10 AM
Category 4 Hurricane Harvey made landfall Friday Night over the northern end of San Jose Island about 4 miles east of Rockport.
Widespread major damage reported in Sat Patricio, Arnasas and Refugio counties.
Data
LOCATION: 28.9N 97.3W or about 25 miles west of Victoria
Maximum sustained winds: 75 mph
Present movement: North at 2 mph
Minimum central pressure: 984 mb or 29.06 inches
Most Current
- Widespread major damage being reported in the cities of Port Aransas, Aransas Pass and Rockport.
- Scattered major damage reported in Portland
- Harvey is expected to stall as it moves inland Saturday. Due to its slow speed, widespread heavy rains are likely north and east of the center of circulation.
Wind Impacts
Corpus Christi- Sustained winds of 30 to 50 MPH will occur through most of Saturday.
Storm Surge 
- There could still be some surge north of Port Aransas, most of this danger is now over.
Additional
The threat of major river flooding exists, especially north and east of Corpus Christi, in Refugio, Goliad, Calhoun and Victoria counties, several inches of rain will fall. Almost all rivers in the area WILL FLOOD over the next several days.
